Everything you need to know about Lysulin — the science, the products, and how to get started.

What is Lysulin and how does it work?

Lysulin is a patented dietary supplement developed by Dr. John Burd Ph.D containing three key nutrients: Lysine, Zinc, and Vitamin C. These ingredients work synergistically to support healthy blood sugar levels by binding excess glucose, enhancing insulin function, and protecting cells from oxidative stress caused by blood sugar fluctuations. The formula is protected by 7 granted patents and has been clinically demonstrated to support healthy A1c levels.

Is Lysulin clinically proven?

Yes. The Lysulin formula has been clinically studied and the individual ingredients have extensive peer-reviewed research behind them. The specific combination is protected by 7 granted patents, reflecting its novelty and scientific validity. Dr. Burd's research background — including founding Dexcom, the world's leading continuous glucose monitor company — underpins the scientific rigor of Lysulin's development.

What makes Lysine, Zinc, and Vitamin C special for blood sugar?

Lysine competitively binds to the same protein sites that glucose attaches to — this process, called glycation, is what drives A1c levels higher. By occupying those sites, Lysine helps block unnecessary glycation.

Zinc is an essential cofactor in over 300 enzymatic reactions including those involved in insulin synthesis, secretion, and receptor sensitivity — directly supporting how efficiently your cells absorb glucose.

Vitamin C shares the same cellular transport channels as glucose. Higher Vitamin C levels competitively reduce glucose uptake by cells, and it also acts as a potent antioxidant against the free radicals that blood sugar spikes generate.

Is Lysulin safe to take with diabetes medication?

Lysulin is made from naturally occurring nutrients (Lysine, Zinc, and Vitamin C) with well-established safety profiles. However, if you are currently taking prescription medication for diabetes or any other condition, we strongly recommend consulting your physician before adding any new supplement to your regimen. Your doctor can help monitor your levels and adjust medication if your blood sugar improves significantly.

Are there any side effects?

Lysulin contains only naturally occurring nutrients at amounts that are well within safe daily intake ranges. No significant side effects have been reported in clinical use. As with any supplement, some individuals with sensitive stomachs may experience mild GI discomfort when starting — taking Lysulin with food typically eliminates this. If you experience any unusual symptoms, discontinue use and consult a healthcare professional.
